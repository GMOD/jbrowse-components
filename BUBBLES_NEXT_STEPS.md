## Consider: identity from bubbles

Currently identity is computed from segment overlap ratio in the segment-based
renderer. With bubbles, we could compute more accurate identity by counting
match/mismatch bases from the bubble CS data. This would give true base-level
identity rather than segment-level approximation.

## GfaTabix vs VCF: what does the graph view offer beyond VCF?

The `vg deconstruct` VCF is the intermediate that drives bubbles, so a fair
question is whether the GfaTabix synteny view offers value over just using a
standard multi-sample VCF viewer. Key differences:

- **Structural context:** The synteny view shows how genomes share or diverge at
  the graph segment level — inversions, large deletions, and rearrangements are
  visible as colored blocks, not just variant records. VCF is site-centric; the
  synteny view is alignment-centric.
- **All-vs-all comparison:** The synteny view renders every genome as a row with
  segment-level coloring, giving an immediate visual of population structure. VCF
  shows genotypes per sample but requires the user to mentally reconstruct how
  samples relate.
- **Base-level + structural in one view:** At zoom-out you see structural
  variation; zoom in and SNPs appear from bubbles. VCF only shows the variant
  sites themselves.
- **Where VCF wins:** Filtering (QUAL, INFO fields), allele frequency stats,
  genotype queries, integration with existing VCF tooling (bcftools, GATK).

The two views are complementary — the synteny view for spatial/structural
context, the VCF track for detailed variant annotation. Both are now
auto-generated by `gfa-to-tabix --bubbles --output-config`.

## Potential improvements

- **Bubble-derived identity coloring:** Use bubble CS match/mismatch counts
  instead of segment overlap ratio for the identity color scheme. This would
  be more accurate at high zoom levels.
- **Variant density heatmap:** At intermediate zoom levels (too far for
  individual SNPs, too close for pure structural view), render a density
  heatmap of variant sites per window.
- **Phasing visualization:** The bubbles now carry per-haplotype allele
  assignments. Could color haplotype 1 vs 2 differently to show phasing
  patterns across samples.
- **Cross-linking:** Click a colored mark in the synteny view to highlight the
  corresponding variant in the VCF track, and vice versa.
